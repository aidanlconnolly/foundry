Go-to-market is *how customers find and buy your product*, and the motion you choose shapes your whole company — your pricing, hiring, metrics, and even product. The first job is to pick the right motion for *your* product, then go deep on the one channel that works before diversifying.

## The three motions

<Framework name="GTM motions">
- **Product-led (PLG).** The product sells itself: free tier or trial, self-serve onboarding, viral/expansion loops. Works when users reach value *fast and alone* (Figma, Notion, dev tools). Low touch, scales cheaply, but needs a product that's genuinely easy to adopt.
- **Sales-led.** Humans sell to humans. Necessary for large, complex, multi-stakeholder purchases (enterprise software, regulated industries). Higher contract values and longer cycles; requires reps, a pipeline, and process.
- **Community-led.** An engaged community or creator ecosystem drives growth through evangelism and content (Notion templates, developer communities). Powerful and durable, but slow to build and hard to manufacture.
</Framework>

These aren't mutually exclusive forever — mature companies blend them — but **early, you pick a primary motion** and let it dictate your priorities.

## How to choose

The motion follows from your product and customer:

- **Price & complexity** — Cheap, simple, fast-to-value → PLG. Expensive, complex, committee-bought → sales-led.
- **Who decides** — An individual can adopt it → PLG. A buying committee must approve → sales-led.
- **Time to value** — Minutes → PLG can work. Months of implementation → sales-led.
- **Where your users gather** — A passionate niche community → community-led can be your wedge.

A useful 2026 note: many AI products are PLG-friendly because value is immediate, but enterprise AI buyers increasingly demand a sales-and-trust motion. Match reality, not fashion.

## Go deep before going wide

<Pull>Early traction almost always comes from one channel working, not five channels dabbling. Find the one, exhaust it, then add the next.</Pull>

The common failure is spraying effort across SEO, paid ads, content, partnerships, and outbound simultaneously — none reaching the focus needed to work. Pick the single channel most aligned with your motion, go deep enough to actually learn whether it works, and only diversify once one is reliably producing customers. Founder-led selling/community-building comes first in every motion; you personally prove the channel before you systematize or hire for it.

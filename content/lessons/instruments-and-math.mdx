You don't need to be a finance whiz, but you must understand the instruments and arithmetic of fundraising — because these terms quietly determine how much of your company you keep and what happens when you exit. (Education, not legal/financial advice; use a lawyer for real docs.)

## The core math: dilution

The arithmetic that governs everything:

<Framework name="Dilution math">
- **Post-money = pre-money + amount raised.**
- **New investor's % = amount raised ÷ post-money.**
- **Existing holders are scaled by** pre-money ÷ post-money.

Example: raise **$2M on $8M pre** → post = **$10M** → investors own **$2M/$10M = 20%**, and everyone before is diluted by 20%.
</Framework>

Model your real plan in the Toolkit's cap-table calculator — seeing founder ownership drop across seed → A → B makes the abstract concrete and helps you avoid over-raising (and over-diluting) for a vanity valuation.

## SAFEs vs priced rounds

- **SAFE** (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) — The standard early instrument. Investor gives cash now for equity *later*, converting at the next priced round per a **valuation cap** and/or **discount**. No interest, no maturity, minimal legal cost. Fast and founder-friendly for pre-seed/seed. Watch your **total SAFE overhang** — stacked SAFEs with low caps can dilute you more than you realize when they all convert.
- **Priced round** — Investors buy shares at an agreed price now, setting an explicit valuation, with full legal docs and usually a board seat. Standard from Series A on.

## The terms that actually matter

Valuation gets the attention, but other terms can matter more:

- **Liquidation preference.** What investors get *before* common shareholders in an exit. **1x non-participating** is the founder-friendly standard: investors take the *greater of* their money back or their as-converted share — not both. **Participating** or **>1x** preferences let them take their money back *and* a share of the rest — they can quietly gut founder/employee proceeds in a modest exit.
- **Option pool.** Often created/expanded *pre-round*, so founders absorb the dilution (the "option pool shuffle"). It effectively lowers your real pre-money — negotiate the size.
- **Pro rata.** Investors' right to keep their % by investing in later rounds. Standard for good early investors.
- **Board composition & control.** Who sits on the board and what needs their approval. Easy to under-weight; hard to undo.

<Pull>A high valuation with a participating 2x preference and a punitive option pool can be a worse deal than a lower valuation with clean 1x non-participating terms. Read past the headline number.</Pull>

## The takeaway

Headline valuation is one variable among several. Understand the dilution math so you don't over-raise, prefer **clean standard terms** (1x non-participating, reasonable pool, standard pro rata) over a flashy valuation with ugly structure, and model every scenario before you sign. The founders who get burned usually optimized the number on the press release and ignored the terms underneath it.
